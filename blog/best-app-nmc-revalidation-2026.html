If you're a nurse, midwife or nursing associate in the UK, keeping on top of your NMC revalidation can feel like one more thing on an already full plate. Between shifts, study days, reflective accounts and CPD hours, it's easy to let things slide until the deadline creeps up on you.

The good news? There are now several apps designed specifically to make this process easier. Whether you're looking for a simple CPD tracker or a full portfolio management tool, 2026 has more options than ever.

In this guide, we'll compare the best revalidation apps available right now and show you how to organise your NMC revalidation portfolio digitally — so you can spend less time on admin and more time on what matters.

Why Use an App for NMC Revalidation?

You can absolutely meet your revalidation requirements using the NMC's free forms. Thousands of nurses do it every cycle. But an app offers three advantages that paper can't match:

  1. Track CPD as you go. Log hours throughout the year instead of trying to remember what you did three years ago.
  2. Reflect when it's fresh. The best reflective accounts are written close to the experience, not reconstructed from memory.
  3. Everything in one place. No hunting for lost certificates, no reformatting documents at the last minute.

Plus, with the NMC currently reviewing the Code and revalidation process — with a public consultation planned for September 2026 — the direction of travel is clearly toward digital. Getting comfortable with a digital portfolio now puts you ahead of the curve.

What to Look For in a Revalidation App

Not all apps are created equal. Here are the features that actually matter:

NMC-compliant templates

The app should mirror the NMC's requirements: 450 practice hours (900 for midwives), 35 hours of CPD (20 participatory), five reflective accounts, and a reflective discussion with your confirmer. If it doesn't map to these, it misses the point.

Fast CPD logging

You should be able to log an activity in under 60 seconds. Date, type, hours, a brief reflective note — done.

Portfolio export

You'll need to share your portfolio with your confirmer. The app should produce a clean, complete document (usually PDF) that you can email or print.

Reminders

An app that tells you when you're falling behind on CPD hours or when your revalidation window is opening is worth its weight in gold.

Fair pricing

You're paying for this yourself. Anything north of £25 a year should offer real added value.

Best Apps for NMC Revalidation in 2026

Here's our roundup of what's currently available for UK nurses and midwives:

FourteenFish — £15/year

FourteenFish is the most established option. It's used by several NHS trusts and has a solid reputation. The portfolio export is clean, the CPD tracking is straightforward, and it integrates with some employer systems. At £15 a year, it's good value.

Best for: Nurses who want a reliable, proven tool with NHS familiarity.

Revalidator.uk — £2 to start

Revalidator.uk is a newer entrant with a voice-powered CPD logging feature — you speak your reflective note and it transcribes it. It's NMC-compliant and the entry price is low. The trade-off is it's still building its feature set.

Best for: Nurses who want voice-to-text convenience at minimal cost.

CPD Launchpad — Free tier available

CPD Launchpad offers a free online portfolio with basic features and a paid tier for more functionality. It's a good entry point if you're not sure you want to commit financially.

Best for: Budget-conscious nurses testing the waters.

RCNi Revalidation Portfolio — Free for RCN members

If you're an RCN member, this is included in your membership. It integrates with RCN Learn courses and sends reminder alerts. Non-members can't access it.

Best for: RCN members who want everything in one ecosystem.

NMC-Revalidation.org — Free

A simple, no-frills online tracker. It covers the basics — CPD log, reflective accounts, PDF export — and costs nothing. The interface is dated but functional.

Best for: Nurses who want free and simple.

Built specifically for UK nurses, Revalidation Copilot focuses on reducing the time it takes to prepare your portfolio. How to Organise Your NMC Revalidation Portfolio Digitally

Making the switch from paper to digital doesn't have to be complicated. Here's a five-step plan:

  1. Choose your app. Pick one from this guide and create an account. Most have a free tier or trial, so test before committing.
  2. Backfill your current cycle. Log everything you've done so far — every CPD activity, your reflective accounts, your practice hours. This is the most time-consuming step, but once it's done, maintenance is minimal.
  3. Log as you go. From now on, log CPD within a day or two of completing it. A brief note while it's fresh beats writing six reflective accounts from scratch later.
  4. Share with your confirmer. Export your portfolio as a PDF or share a link. Your confirmer can review everything digitally without a stack of paper.
  5. Submit via MyNMC. Your app handles the organisation. The submission still goes through the NMC's online portal at nmc.org.uk.
