The Confirmer's Guide to the New Revalidation

Published 25 July 2026 · Updated 25 July 2026

If you manage or employ nurses, midwives, or nursing associates, the revalidation changes approved by the NMC Council on 21 July 2026 will directly affect you. Line managers are now the default confirmers, the feedback form is becoming mandatory, and the reflective discussion is getting more structured.

This guide is written for you: the employers, line managers, and HR leads who will be supporting staff through revalidation over the next two years and beyond.

The big picture: The NMC's proposals include an increased role for confirmers and reflective discussion partners, a closer link to the Code and Standards, and a refreshed approach to verification. Nursing Times reports there will be a "mandatory form that focuses on what professionals learned and how they improved."

What the new mandatory feedback form requires

The optional feedback template is becoming mandatory. This is a significant change. Under the current system, confirmers complete a template that asks whether the professional has met revalidation requirements and whether there are any concerns about their fitness to practise.

The new form shifts the focus. Instead of a compliance checklist, it centres on what the professional learned and how they improved their practice. Nursing Times reports the NMC wants a "mandatory form that focuses on what professionals learned and how they improved their practice."

Source: NMC official news release, 21 July 2026 and Nursing Times

How to conduct a confirmation discussion

The confirmation discussion is not a tick-box exercise. It is a professional conversation about the registrant's practice, development, and reflections. Under the new proposals, this discussion becomes even more important because the feedback form will ask about learning and improvement.

Here is a practical framework for the discussion:

Before the meeting. Ask the registrant to share their portfolio, reflective accounts, and CPD log in advance. Review them before the discussion. This is not something you should do in the meeting itself.

During the meeting. Focus on three areas: what they learned from their CPD, how they applied that learning in practice, and what they will do differently going forward. Use their reflective accounts as a starting point for the conversation.

After the meeting. Complete the feedback form promptly while the discussion is fresh. Be specific about examples of improvement you discussed.

What employers need to prepare for

The revalidation changes are expected from April 2028. Between now and then, here is what you can do to prepare:

  • Understand the confirmer role. Ensure line managers know they are the default confirmers and understand what the role involves.
  • Build capacity. If you have many staff with revalidation due in the same period, plan for the workload of confirmation discussions.
  • Update policies. Your appraisal and revalidation support policies may need updating to reflect the new mandatory feedback form.
  • Support staff. Encourage your teams to keep their portfolios up to date throughout the year, not just in the weeks before their deadline.

Practical checklist for managers

  • [ ] Know your staff's revalidation dates. Plan confirmation discussions at least four weeks before their deadline.
  • [ ] Review the registrant's portfolio before the discussion, not during it.
  • [ ] Prepare questions that explore learning and improvement, not just compliance.
  • [ ] Complete the feedback form as soon as possible after the discussion.
  • [ ] Keep a record of confirmation discussions for your own professional governance.
  • [ ] Be aware of the 12-week extension option for staff with valid needs.
