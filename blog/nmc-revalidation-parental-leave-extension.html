Revalidation After Maternity Leave or Career Break: New 2026 Rules

Published 29 July 2026 · Updated 29 July 2026

Returning to nursing after maternity leave, parental leave, a career break, or long-term sick leave comes with enough challenges. Revalidation should not be one of them. The NMC's new proposals include changes that make it easier to complete revalidation when you have been away from practice.

Two things in the NMC's 21 July 2026 announcement matter most for nurses returning from leave. First, the 12-week extension for professionals with a valid need such as ill health, parental leave, or bereavement. Second, more tailored support and guidance for professionals with different roles and scopes of practice.

What's relevant for you: A 12-week extension (up from 8 weeks) for valid needs like parental leave, ill health, or bereavement. Plus the NMC is committed to more tailored guidance for professionals in different situations.

The new 12-week extension explained

If you are on maternity leave when your revalidation deadline arrives, or you return from leave with only a few weeks to complete everything, the 12-week extension gives you breathing room. The NMC's Council papers describe this as improving access to revalidation for professionals who face genuine barriers.

The extension applies to professionals with a valid need. The NMC has specifically mentioned:

  • Parental leave (maternity, paternity, adoption, shared parental leave)
  • Ill health (your own serious or long-term illness)
  • Bereavement (death of a close family member or dependent)

Source: NMC official news release, 21 July 2026

How to plan your CPD and reflections around a return to practice

Here is a practical plan for completing revalidation when you are returning from leave:

Check your deadline first. Log into NMC Online and confirm your revalidation date. If it falls during or shortly after your leave, you will need to request an extension. Do this before your deadline passes.

Audit what you already have. If you have been on leave for less than 12 months, you may already have some practice hours and CPD from before your leave. Count what you have, work out what you still need.

Plan your CPD around your return. Your CPD does not all need to be formal courses. Reading journal articles, attending in-service training, completing e-learning modules, and reflecting on your return-to-practice induction all count. Use your first weeks back to build CPD around the practical learning of re-entering practice.

Write reflections as you go. The new mandatory feedback form will ask about learning and improvement. Your reflective accounts are the best evidence of this. Write them when things are fresh, not at the last minute.

Arrange your reflective discussion early. Your confirmer is now expected to be your line manager by default. If you are returning to a new role or manager, have the conversation about confirmation early so they know what is required.

What counts as a valid need

The NMC will define the exact criteria during the consultation from September to December 2026. Based on the Council papers, a valid need is a genuine circumstance that prevents you from completing revalidation on time. It is not a convenience option.

The examples given are clear: parental leave, ill health, bereavement. If any of these apply to you, the extension should be straightforward.

Tailored support from the NMC

The NMC has committed to providing more tailored guidance for professionals with different roles and scopes of practice. This includes professionals returning from leave. Expect more practical resources and clearer signposting for how revalidation applies in different circumstances.
