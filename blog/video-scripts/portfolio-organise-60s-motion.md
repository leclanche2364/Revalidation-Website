# Motion Graphics Script: "How to Organise Your NMC Revalidation Portfolio"

**Format:** 60-second motion graphics with voiceover
**Tone:** Direct, calm, problem-solution. One human voice. No rushed delivery.
**Target:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
**Music:** Low-tempo electronic pulse throughout. Voice is the lead. Music drops slightly during Scene 3 (the truth), returns at Scene 6 (the solution).

---

## SCENE 1 — The Scramble (0:00–0:09)

**Visual:** Split screen. Left side: chaotic desktop — browser tabs, emails, crumpled paper. Right side: a calendar with "REVALIDATION DUE" circled in red. Papers start flying across the screen.

**VO:**
> Six weeks before your deadline, you open your laptop. And you realise you stopped tracking... about two years ago.

**On-screen text (appears staggered):**
> SIX WEEKS LEFT
> YOUR DEADLINE →
> YOU STOPPED TRACKING [2 YEARS AGO]

## Production Notes

**Pacing:** The voiceover is deliberately unhurried. 115 words in 60 seconds is a comfortable speaking rate. Do not speed it up.

**Voice:** Male or female, calm, grounded. Think "competent colleague explaining something over a coffee" rather than "advertising voice." Avoid a high-energy announcer tone — this audience is tired and wants relief, not hype.

**Colour palette:** Dark navy background (#0c2b3b) for the serious/problem scenes. Shift to teal (#1b7e6f) for the app/solution scenes. The chaotic opening uses warmer, muted tones to create contrast.

**Typography:** Clean sans-serif (Inter). Large, bold key numbers (450, 35, 5, 60). The numbers should be the visual anchor — nurses scan for numbers when time is tight.

**Scene 4 (the checklist):** This is the most information-dense scene. Each line gets 1–1.3 seconds. The voiceover should match the pace of the visuals — one number, one pause, next number.

**Music:** Low-tempo electronic pulse throughout. Consistent volume — do not dip during voiceover. End on a clean, resolved note.

**30-second alternative:** Scenes 1→4→6→7→9. Trim the checklist scene to just the headline.
